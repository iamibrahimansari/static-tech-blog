Write a program in C programming language to find / calculate area of given circle up-to 3 digits after decimal point, if area contains non zero digit otherwise convert it into an integer number?

The area of a circle is the space occupied by the circle in a two-dimensional plane. Alternatively, the space occupied within the boundary/circumference of a circle is called the area of the circle. The formula for the area of a circle is A = πr2, where r is the radius of the circle. π = 22 / 7.

You are given radius of circle. Your job is to find area of given circle. If area of circle doesn't contain any non-zero digits after decimal point(ex: 13.000000) then print "13" otherwise print area up to 3 digits after decimal point. Area can't be negative that's why if user enter 0 or negative values for radius then print "Your input is invalid." on screen.

Example 1

Input

Enter radius of given circle: 32.67

Output

Area of given circle is 3354.462

Explanation

Area of Circle = (22 / 7) x radius x radius. Here radius is 32.67 which is converted implicitly into 32.670000 by compiler that's why (22/7) x 32.670000 x 32.670000 = 3354.462257 but we have to display area only up to 3 digits after decimal point that's why we get 3354.462

Example 2

Input

Enter radius of given circle: 7

Output

Area of given circle is 154

Explanation

Area of Circle = (22 / 7) x radius x radius. Here radius is 7 which is converted implicitly into 7.000000 by compiler that's why (22/7) x 7.000000 x 7.000000 = 154.000000 but we have converted into integer that's why we get 154

Example 2

Input

Enter radius of given circle: 0

Output

Your input is invalid.

Explanation

Radius of circle can't be negative that's why user input which is 0 is invalid.
